## Point 1) Giving to God must always be in our mind

Again, let’s turn our Bibles to **2 Corinthians 9:1**. The last part of the verse says:

> “There is no need for me to write to you…”
> 

That means they already had giving in their minds.

Paul is saying, “I don’t need to remind you.” Why? Because giving had already taken a place in their thinking. It was not something they were forced to do, it was something they had already decided.

Are we together brethren?

Now let us bring it to ourselves.

If every time it is time to give, we start asking, “What will I give today?” then it means giving is not yet in our mind. But when something is important to us, we prepare for it.

For example, when we know school fees are coming, we don’t wait for the last day. We plan, we arrange, we keep something aside. Why? Because it is already in our mind.

In the same way, giving to God must be intentional. It must be something we think about even before we come to church.

Hallelujah.

So we must begin to train ourselves. Even when we receive something during the week, we ask ourselves, “What part of this belongs to God?”

Are we together?

Because if giving is not first in our mind, it will never be in our hand.

Let us say to ourselves: we will not wait to be reminded, we will not give by chance, we will prepare our hearts to give.

Amen.

May the Lord help us in Jesus name.

Alright, let’s continue in the same flow and spirit.


## Point  number 2) We must always be eager to give

Let us go back to our main text, **2 Corinthians 9:2a**. The Bible says:

> “For I know your eagerness to help…”
> 

Paul says, “I know your eagerness.”

That means their eagerness was not hidden. It was something that could be seen. It was something that could be proven.

Are we together brethren?

This tells us that eagerness is not just something we say, it is something that shows in our actions.

You don’t need to announce that you are eager to give. People will know. It will reflect in the way you respond when there is a need.

Hallelujah.

Now let us bring it to ourselves.

When it is time to give, how do we respond?

Do we delay?

Do we complain?

Do we start calculating too much?

Or do we respond with a willing heart?

Because the Bible says:

> “For I know your eagerness…”
> 

Meaning eagerness can be known.

Are we together?

If someone looks at our life, can they say, “This person is eager to support the work of God”?

Or will they say, “This one must always be begged”?

Let us be honest with ourselves.


Let me give us a simple example.

When something concerns us personally, we don’t delay. If there is an emergency in the family, we act quickly. We don’t start negotiating or postponing.

Why?

Because our heart is there.

In the same way, when our heart is truly with God, we will not struggle to give. We will not be forced. We will not wait until we are pushed.

Giving will come from a place of willingness.

Amen.

## Point number 3) We must always be ready to defend our testimony in giving

Let us look again at our main text, **2 Corinthians 9:2b and verse 4**. The Bible says:

> “I have been boasting about it  to the Macedonians…”
> 

And in verse 4:

> “We—not to say anything about you—would be ashamed of having been so confident.”
> 

The Great apostle Paul is saying something very serious here.

He said, “I have been boasting it about to the Macedonians .” That means he was confidently telling others about their giving. He was using them as an example.

Are we together brethren?

But then he adds something in verse 4, he says if they are not ready, “we would be ashamed.”

Meaning their testimony had to match what was said about them.

Hallelujah.

Can someone speak about our giving without fear of being disappointed?

Can someone say, “I know this brother, I know this sister, i know this church or congregation when it comes to supporting the work of God, they will stand”?

Or will they be afraid to mention our name?

Let us think about it.

Because our giving is not just between us and God alone, it also becomes a testimony to others.

Are we together?

Paul said their zeal had already encouraged others. Their giving was not only helping needs, it was inspiring people.

That is powerful.


Let me share a short story.

There was a woman in a community who was not very rich, but she was known for one thing: whenever there was a need in church, she would always be among the first to respond.

Over time, people began to notice her consistency. One day, a young man who was struggling said, “If this woman, with little, can still give, then what is my excuse?”

That testimony changed him. He began to give as well.

Brethren, her giving was speaking.

Hallelujah.


This is what God is calling us to.

A life where our giving becomes a testimony. A life where others can be encouraged because of us.

So that when someone speaks about us, they are not guessing, they are confident.

Are we together?

Let us challenge ourselves today that :

We will not have a weak testimony.

We will not disappoint those who trust our commitment.

We will live in such a way that our giving can be spoken of boldly.

## Point number 4) We must be ready to actually give

Let us go back to our main text, **2 Corinthians 9:3**. The Bible says:

> “But I am sending the brothers in order that our boasting about you in this matter should not prove hollow, but that you may be ready, as I said you would be.”
> 

Paul says, “that you may be ready.”

Are we together brethren?

This is very important.

Because they already had:

- the mindset
- the eagerness
- the testimony

But Paul is still saying, “Be ready.”

Hallelujah.

Why?

Because it is possible to:

- think about giving
- talk about giving
- even be known for giving

…and still not actually give when the time comes.

Are we together?

This is where many of us struggle.

We have good intentions.

We make promises.

We say, “Next time I will do better…”

But when the opportunity comes, we are not ready.

The Bible says:

> “That you may be ready…”
> 

Meaning when the moment comes, there should be no delay, no excuse, no withdrawal.

Hallelujah.

For example.

Someone can promise to support a project. Everyone is happy, everyone is encouraged. But when the time comes to fulfill it, the person is nowhere to be found.

Brethren, that is what Paul wanted to avoid.

He did not want their words to be empty. He did not want their testimony to collapse.


That is why he prepared them ahead of time.

So that when the moment comes, their giving will match their promise.

Are we together?

The Bible also tells us:

> “Faith without works is dead.” (James 2:17)
> 

In the same way, we can say:

Giving without action is empty.


So we must speak to ourselves today.

We will not only plan.

We will not only talk.

We will not only promise.

We will be ready to act.

Hallelujah.


When the time to give comes, we will be prepared.

When the opportunity comes, we will respond.

When God places a need before us, we will not withdraw.


Are we together brethren?

May the Lord help us to move from intention to action, in Jesus name.
